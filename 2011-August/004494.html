It should not be too difficult to have a native SVG renderer (instead of 
using Cairo) for Mapnik. After all, all a renderer has to do is to 
accept a geo feature, iterate over the styles, and output rendering 
instructions... or something ;)

In fact, Dane recently told me that (quote)

 >>>>
Yes, a native svg_renderer was started and is in mapnik2, just disabled 
by default and I think requires something like >= boost 1.43 or 1.44 to 
compile properly.

http://trac.mapnik.org/wiki/MapnikRenderers#svg_renderer
<<<<

Quote from the trac page, "Only the basics are implemented at this 
point...".

I had a potential client who wanted AI-editable SVG and I was planning

I'm also still interested and could conceivably spend a little time on 
it, or maybe you could re-activate Carlos López Garcés, the original author,

If you're looking for AI compatiblity, you should also talk to Igor

distict output modes, one for normal SVG and one for AI SVG.
