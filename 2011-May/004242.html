wrote:
> We are currently investigating whether Mapnik can be used in our
software
> stack. One of the requirements is map rotation. It must be possible to
> rotate the map from within the application that is being developed.
> To my knowledge this is currently not supported in Mapnik, however I did
> saw it on the roadmap for version 2.0. Can you give me an indication on
> when Mapnik 2.0 will probably be released? Or is there another solution
to
> this problem for instance a patch?

I worked on this one and a half years ago. There was no direct support of
map rotation in mapnik then, and I had to adopt a rather tedious manual
way, by rotation of all data sources around the same point, and then
rendering the map with the resulting fake coordinates. Some coarse details
are given at the bottom of http://www.ancalime.de/rotated.html . Rotation
of vector data in PostGIS was easy, as this has support for affine
transformations. The most difficult part turned out to be the rotation of
raster data, as not all converted data could be read in by mapnik again. If
you are interested, I could discuss this in more detail and also provide
code snippets in ruby (this is just part of a larger system, so you would
need to modify and integrate it yourself).
