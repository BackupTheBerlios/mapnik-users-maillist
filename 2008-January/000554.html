On 08/01/2008, Paul Smith <paulsmith at pobox.com> wrote:
>
> I have a street blocks shapefile (the blocks are polylines), and the
> blocks are rendered with the line symbolizer. When I try to label the
> lines with "LINE_PLACEMENT", if the length of the label as drawn is
> greater than the length of the line, the label is not drawn. What I'd
> prefer is that the label be drawn even if it extends beyond the
> endpoints of the line, because the blocks are dense and relatively
> short, so very few labels actually get drawn at a readable font size.
> (POINT_PLACEMENT is not an option for the application.) What are my
> options?


So if i understand this correctly you want it drawn following the border of
the block, yet there are lots of dense boxes and the label placer will
decide which edge(s) of each block any label is drawn on? Doesn't sound very
appealing to me, but hey ;)
And drawing it in the centroid of the block (point placement) is no good?

You can do point placement with calculated geometries, so having it drawn in
the center of the 'top left quadrant' of your blocks wouldn't be difficult
either... (or bottom right corner, or centered along the top, etc). Look at
the anchor property of the textsymbolizer first before you break out your
geometry-manipulation functions.
