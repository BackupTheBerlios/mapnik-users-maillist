Vladimir Slepnev wrote:
> Yeah, we tried pngs. They're huge. 

hmm -- well, they would be huge if you have something photographic as 
your background, but they shouldn't be for computer rendered stuff.

I would at least do some comparisons though -- if the text looks good 
with PNG, then Mapnik is not at fault. You'll then need to decide size 
vs. quality.

If you do have photos as a background, you may be able to use jpeg for 
those, then a separate transparent image on top in PNG for the roads, 
text, etc, to get best of both worlds.

(Microsoft uses them though) Also
> we tried maximum JPEG quality, text stays blurry.

JPEG is "lossy" and not well suited to text. That's really all there is 
to it.

> All right, attaching a screenshot from there.

That does look like jpeg blurriness to me -- at least some of it.

About sub-pixel positioning -- MS does indeed, in Windows, anyway, 
sacrifice pretty for "readable", but they do it in a very non-optimum way.

Maxim Shemanarev, of Agg (which mapnik uses) fame, as done some nice 
work on that:

http://www.antigrain.com/research/font_rasterization/

Are you using any of his suggestions in Mapnik?
