>     So I know what a Map object's zoom_to_box method does, and I know
> what m.zoom_all() does, but I can't figure out what these methods do,
> even after playing around with them for a bit:


All these methods for convenience. You can emulate same behavior with  
recalculating bounding box.
>
>      * m.zoom()

zoom (factor) :  In bounding box terms this method will shrink or  
enlarge bounding box by factor. If factor=2 bounding box will grow
while size of the map stays the same.
>      * m.pan()

pan (x,y) : moves view port  by x and y (paper coordinates) relative  
to the centre e.g
pan(0.5 * map.width,0)


>      * m.pan_and_zoom()
>

Just a combination of two.
I added new ticket to the trac to add/improve doc strings.
