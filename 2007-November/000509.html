Hi Paul,

> Is there any interest in or has there been any discussion about  
> adding a "border" style property to a stroke? Consider the common  
> case of a streets layer. Often the streets are represented by  
> lines, and it is usual in the rendered map to display the streets  
> with some width and a border to approximate the real-world width  
> dimension. The only current way that I know of to get a border in  
> this case is by adding an additional style below the street style  
> that is slightly wider (the street width minus the new style width  
> divided by two equals the border width) and drawn with a different  
> color (the border color). Despite being hackish and affecting  
> performance, it creates unexpected results, for example, in layouts  
> with transparency on the streets layer (both the street and the  
> "border" styles would need to be transparent, and the color of the  
> "border" would bleed through and add into the streets color).
This is why 'Hybrid' layer in GMap doesn't have 'casing' I guess.
>
>
> I could imagine the interface for this looking something like this  
> (in the XML, for example):
>
> <CssParameter name="stroke-border-width">2</CssParameter>
> <CssParameter name="stroke-border-color">#999999</CssParameter>
>
> It would inherit the stroke's opacity, line join & cap, and dash  
> array.

Well, it could have its own opacity and dash etc.
>
>
> Is this even possible with the agg renderer? (Haven't investigated  
> that ...)

Yes, I think it'll be possible to implement. I don't think there'll  
be any performance gains, in fact the opposite is likely.
We need to investigate pros and cons. Perhaps this can be an option.  
Do you mind submitting a ticket to trac.mapnik.org?
