Is there any interest in or has there been any discussion about adding a
"border" style property to a stroke? Consider the common case of a streets
layer. Often the streets are represented by lines, and it is usual in the
rendered map to display the streets with some width and a border to
approximate the real-world width dimension. The only current way that I know
of to get a border in this case is by adding an additional style below the
street style that is slightly wider (the street width minus the new style
width divided by two equals the border width) and drawn with a different
color (the border color). Despite being hackish and affecting performance,
it creates unexpected results, for example, in layouts with transparency on
the streets layer (both the street and the "border" styles would need to be
transparent, and the color of the "border" would bleed through and add into
the streets color).

I could imagine the interface for this looking something like this (in the
XML, for example):

<CssParameter name="stroke-border-width">2</CssParameter>
<CssParameter name="stroke-border-color">#999999</CssParameter>

It would inherit the stroke's opacity, line join & cap, and dash array.

Is this even possible with the agg renderer? (Haven't investigated that ...)
