As part of the Google Summer of Code and it's Mentor's Summit at the end of
the year, Google is sponsoring a "documentation sprint". It sounds like
they're looking for people interested in writing documentation for open
source projects AND projects to write documentation for. It sounds like, if
accepted, Google will pay most of your travel costs and all of
your accommodation (hotel + food).

I'm planning on sending in an application for OpenStreetMap to be considered
as one of those organizations, but I'm also interested in hearing from
anyone in the OSM community that would be interested in writing
documentation.

More details are here:
https://sites.google.com/site/docsprintsummit/

Let me know if you're interested,
Ian
