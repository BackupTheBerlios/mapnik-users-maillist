Hi all,


With the deadline for student proposals approaching, I'd like to put
in my candidacy for implementing the idea I put on the trac regarding
the php binding for mapnik. I put multiple ideas there but given the
number of excellent other proposals, I figured it's best that I
restrict myself to the one I feel most excited about, which is the php
extension.

As I described, I think a php binding would be of great benefit to
mapnik, to get it more widely used. I realize it's more broadening
mapnik's scope rather than deepening the core mapnik functionality
like some of the other proposed GSoC projects are, but I don't think
that detracts from its potential.

The first thing I would focus on is extending the functionality to
cover the whole range of mapnik functionality, and making it
compatible with mapnik2, also. A production quality binding needs a
full-coverage test suite and it needs a build toolchain that
integrates with the way other php extensions are build.

A short profile of myself is also in order I think; I'm not the
traditional university student (but I do qualify under the GSoC rules
:) ). I'm part time law student at the university of Maastricht in the
Netherlands, I'm part time dad and part time progammer. I've been
programming professionally since I graduated with an applied
informatics degree in 2000, mostly in C++; but I've written a lot of
php code too. In my current job I work with geodata a lot and I've
written several map rendering implementations over the years (although
with different objectives than mapnik).


regards,

Roel
