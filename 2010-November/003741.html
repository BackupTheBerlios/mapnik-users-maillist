> We are currently rendering OSM using mapnik in Window platform with reasonable spec of h/w (2.6G CPU and 3G RAM). We just want to know usually how long does it takes to render OSM map. Most information about tile generation/seeding has already been said by the others.
But what is also important is the way you are generating these tiles and what seeding tool you are using?

 - Do you make use of multicores?
 - How do you define what you are seeding? A BBOX has a pretty bad hit/miss ration for Japan.
 - Do you seed tile after tile, from top to bottom or some more advanced strategy?

I have written the seeding tool for MapProxy[0] and there are a lot of parts where you can optimize the seeding process, in contrast to naive top to bottom tile generation.

[0] http://mapproxy.org/docs/latest/seed.html
