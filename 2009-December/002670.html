Dane Springmeyer wrote:
> 
> What Mapnik and postgis versions are you running?  Also, how are you  
> rendering tiles?
> 
here are some details of my environment:

- Mapnik 0.6.1
- Postgis 8.3.7
- Debian Lenny
- Nik2Img 0.4.0
- Tilecache 2.0.4
- QuadCore-CPU
- 4GB RAM

The errors occurs in every situation: The examples above are rendered with
nik2img. I generate the map tiles with Tilecache (Metatile=20,20) and a
tileseed script. I'm also running an OGCserver with access to my XML-style.
Everytime the same error.

My XML-style has about 10700 lines with 81 styles and 110 layers for 3
countries and I can't reduce this. The vector data is stored in EPSG:4326.


Jon Burgess-2 wrote:
> 
> The general solution is to set estimated_extent to false and set the
> extent to include all your data, or -180,-90,180,90 if you have the
> whole world.
> 

Hey, that's it! I never tried this, because my data extent is much smaller
than this. I tested it again with nik2img. The results: with

	  <Parameter name="estimate_extent">false</Parameter>
	  <Parameter name="extent">-180,-85,180,85</Parameter>

the render time is about 4 seconds and with:

	  <Parameter name="estimate_extent">false</Parameter>

the time increases to over 10 minutes! 
I cannot explain why, but this is the solution. Does anyone know, why this
happens? It would be helpful to understand it in other situations. Is the
parameter "estimate_extent=true" perhaps unnecessary?
